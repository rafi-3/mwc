Ambuyat

300 calories | 15mins

Ambuyat is a dish derived from the interior trunk of the sago palm. It is a starchy bland substance, similar to tapioca starch. Ambuyat is the national dish of Brunei. Ambuyat is eaten with a bamboo fork called a chandas, by rolling the starch around the prongs and then dipping it into a sauce, of which there are many varieties.

HOW TO MAKE AMBUYAT

  1. Sago is mixed with 4 cups of water and fry for 10 minutes.
  2. Pour water and place sago in a hot-water container.
  3. Mix sago with hot boiling water.
  4. until smooth and cook until sago looks clear and viscous (such as the glue. It indicates that the sago is cooked).
ALTERNATIVE WAY
  1. Sago flour mixed with water
  2. Cook on a skillet with a moderate fire until it becomes viscous and solid.

HOW TO MAKE THE SAUCE

  1. Add belacan, chilli beans, lime juice, a little sugar, a little onion with shrimp in a mortar or blender can.
  2. Punch / crush until crushed.
  3. Ready.
